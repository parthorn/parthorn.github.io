Teaching roles and responsibilities

ACM 95/100b: Introductory Methods of Applied Mathematics for the Physical Sciences

Teaching Assistant, Caltech's Department of Computing and Mathematical Sciences (CMS) (Spring 2025)

Teaching assistant for an applied and computational math for science and engineering course with 150+ students. Hosted weekly office hours and prepared and graded problem sets and exams. Topics include eigenvalue problems for second-order ODEs (Sturm-Liouville), Fourier series and transforms, Laplace transforms, classification of second-order PDEs, classical PDEs (heat, wave, Laplace, Poisson, Helmholtz) and their solution techniques, and Green's functions.

EE/APh 40: Physics of Electrical Engineering

Teaching Assistant, Caltech's Department of Electrical Engineering (Winter 2025)

Teaching assistant for a core device-physics EE course with 30+ students. Hosted weekly office hours, designed and graded problem sets and exams, and put together materials to help students prepare for exams. Topics include device-physics fundamentals (electromagnetics, semiconductors, waves), transmission line theory, Maxwell's equations, piezoelectricity, electrodynamics and EM waves, wave-matter interactions, reflection and transmission, lasers, waveguides, resonators, semiconductor physics and pn junction, and optoelectronic devices.

EE 44: Deterministic Analysis of Systems and Circuits

Teaching Assistant, Caltech's Department of Electrical Engineering (Fall 2024)

Teaching assistant for one of Caltech's core undergraduate EE courses with 30+ students. Hosted weekly office hours, prepared and graded problem sets and exams, and organized review sessions to help students prepare for exams. Topics include mathematical modeling of physical systems focusing on electrical systems, deterministic system analysis methods, matrix representations, time-domain analysis (impulse and step responses), signal superposition and convolution, Heaviside operator solutions for linear differential equations, transfer functions, Laplace and Fourier transforms, applications in electrical circuits (energy/data converters, communication channels, instrumentation, sensing), and selected applications beyond electrical circuits (public opinion, acoustics, financial markets, traffic, drug delivery, mechanical systems, news cycles, and heat exchange).

Ph 2a: Waves, Quantum Physics, and Statistical Mechanics

Teaching Assistant, Caltech's Division of Physics, Mathematics, and Astronomy (PMA) (Fall 2023)

Teaching assistant for a sophomore physics class with 150+ students. Hosted weekly office hours and recitation sessions with 30+ students. Prepared problem sets and exams. Topics include oscillations, waves, coupled oscillators, diffraction, Fourier analysis, and introduction to quantum mechanics.
