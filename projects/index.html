Experimental Generation of Polarization-Entangled Photons and Verification of Quantum Nonlocality

An Experimental Demostration of The Violation of Bell's Inequality

Built an optical setup to generate polarization-entangled photons using a nonlinear crystal (BBO) and a violet laser. Measured the correlations in the detection events and demonstrated a clear violation of Bell's inequality with more than 40 statdard deviations.

This project was part of a graduate-level course in Advanced Experimental Physics in Spring 2025. Final report available here.

Design of Broadband Low-Noise Transimpedance Amplifier in 65-nm CMOS for Optical Applications

High-Speed Optical Interconnects for Data Communication Systems

Designed a transimpedance amplifier (TIA) in 65-nm CMOS technology for high-speed optical interconnects. Achieved a bandwidth of more than 10 GHz and a transimpedance gain of 77.8 dBΩ. The TIA was designed to operate with a photodiode for optical data communication systems. Simulated the design and verified the results using Cadence Virtuoso and Spectre.

This project was part of a graduate-level course in Analog Circuit Design in Spring 2025. Final report available here.
